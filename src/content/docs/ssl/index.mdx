<Description>

Encrypt your web traffic to prevent data theft and other tampering.

</Description>

<Plan type="all" />

SSL/TLS certificates encrypt traffic between visitors and your website, preventing eavesdropping and data tampering. Because Cloudflare sits between your visitors and your origin server, two certificates can be involved in a single request: an [edge certificate](/ssl/concepts/#edge-certificate) (visitor to Cloudflare) and an [origin certificate](/ssl/concepts/#origin-certificate) (Cloudflare to your server).

Cloudflare automatically issues free certificates through [Universal SSL](/ssl/edge-certificates/universal-ssl/) and offers additional options for custom certificate management. Refer to [Get started](/ssl/get-started/) to set up SSL/TLS for your domain.

## Features

<Feature
	header="Total TLS"
	href="/ssl/edge-certificates/additional-options/total-tls/"
>
	Universal SSL covers your apex domain and first-level subdomains. Total TLS
	extends that coverage by automatically issuing certificates for proxied
	hostnames at any subdomain level.
</Feature>

<Feature
	header="Delegated DCV"
	href="/ssl/edge-certificates/changing-dcv-method/methods/delegated-dcv/"
>
	Before issuing a certificate, a certificate authority (CA) must verify you
	control the domain. If you manage DNS outside of Cloudflare, you can delegate
	this verification to Cloudflare so certificate renewals happen automatically.
</Feature>

<Feature
	header="Custom TLS settings"
	href="/ssl/edge-certificates/additional-options/minimum-tls/"
>
	Specify the minimum TLS version that visitors must use to connect to your
	website or application, and [restrict cipher
	suites](/ssl/edge-certificates/additional-options/cipher-suites/customize-cipher-suites/)
	to meet compliance or security requirements.
</Feature>

<br />

For a complete list of SSL/TLS features and their availability by plan, refer to [features and availability](/ssl/reference/all-features/).
