Cloudflare WAN (formerly Magic WAN) connects your data centers, offices, and cloud resources through Cloudflare's global network. Instead of backhauling traffic through a central data center or maintaining dedicated MPLS circuits at every site, your traffic routes through the nearest Cloudflare data center where security policies apply inline.

Cloudflare WAN provides secure, performant [routing](https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/network-layer/what-is-routing/) for your entire corporate network. [Cloudflare Network Firewall](/cloudflare-network-firewall/) integrates with Cloudflare WAN, enabling you to enforce network firewall policies at Cloudflare's global network, across traffic from any entity within your network.

You connect your sites to Cloudflare through <GlossaryTooltip term="on-ramp">on-ramps</GlossaryTooltip> — tunnels or direct connections from your network to Cloudflare. Cloudflare WAN supports IPsec tunnels, GRE tunnels, [Cloudflare Network Interconnect](/network-interconnect/), and the [Cloudflare One Appliance](/cloudflare-wan/configuration/appliance/) for connecting your sites.

[Cloudflare One](/cloudflare-one/) is the full SASE (Secure Access Service Edge) platform. It extends Cloudflare WAN with identity-aware security services:

- **[Cloudflare One Client (WARP)](/cloudflare-one/team-and-resources/devices/cloudflare-one-client/)** — deploys on user devices to route traffic through Cloudflare with identity context.

- **[Cloudflare Access](/cloudflare-one/access-controls/)** — enforces Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA) policies based on user identity, device posture, and context.

When you need user-level security policies, identity-based access controls, or secure Internet egress, you can add Cloudflare One capabilities to your existing deployment.

Cloudflare One builds on the same network infrastructure as Cloudflare WAN, so there is no migration required.
